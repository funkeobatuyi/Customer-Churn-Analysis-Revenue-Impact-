# 📊 Customer Churn Analysis & Revenue Impact (SaaS Case Study)

## 🧠 Project Overview

A SaaS company noticed a decline in revenue despite continuous customer acquisition.

This project analyzes a dataset of **79,000+ subscribers** to uncover:

- Why customers are churning  
- Which segments are most at risk  
- How churn impacts revenue  
- What strategies can improve retention  

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

The company was acquiring new users but still experiencing revenue decline.

> **Key Question:**  
Why are customers leaving, and how much is it costing the business?

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1️⃣ Early-Stage Retention Gap
- Highest churn occurs within **0–6 months**
- Indicates poor onboarding or delayed value delivery

👉 **Insight:**  
The business is failing to convert new users into long-term customers

---

### 2️⃣ Market Concentration Risk
- Highest churn observed in:
  - United States  
  - India  

👉 **Insight:**  
Targeted retention strategies in these regions can significantly reduce revenue loss

---

### 3️⃣ The “Lindy Effect” in Customer Retention
- Customers who stay longer are less likely to churn  
- Users beyond **18 months** show strong loyalty  

👉 **Insight:**  
Retention stabilizes over time — focus should be on early-stage engagement

---

## 💡 Business Recommendation

### 🚀 “Bridge to Month 7 Strategy”

Focus on improving the first 6 months of the customer journey:

- Enhance onboarding experience  
- Deliver value quickly  
- Improve engagement for new users
